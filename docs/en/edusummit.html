EduSummit

EduSummit

Where Teachers meet Programmers

EduSummit is a special section of the conference focused on teaching programming at primary and secondary schools. After two years of having an EduTrack at PyCon SK, consisting of a single day of talks for teachers, we are now organising a two day summit during which teachers will be introduced to innovative ways and methods of teaching programming, share their knowledge of useful tools and last but not least participate in numerous workshops given by experienced educators.

This year’s keynote speaker will be Juraj Hromkovič, a professor at ETH Zürich, whose main focus of work is education for teachers of Computer Science.

Profesor Juraj Hromkovič, DrSc.

Please note that EduSummit will be primarily in Slovak language.

EduSummit workshops

On saturday we will hold numerous educational workshops.

  • A beginner's workshop with BBC micro:bit given by high school students
  • An advanced workshop with BBC micro:bit for teachers who already teach with micro:bits.

We will update the workshop list after the Call for Proposals is closed.

SPy Cup

Students from all 14 schools currently participating in Teaching with Hardware will join the SPy Cup contest, in which they create their own micro:bit project a create a step-by-step manual which the will publish under an open license on our website to inspire other teachers and students. The best projects will be awarded prizes and their authors will have a short presentation about their project at PyCon SK 2019.

More info can be found at www.ucimeshardverom.sk/sutaz

Hardware Projects Showcase

If you have created an interesting hardware project which uses Python, MicroPython or the BBC micro:bit and would like to share it to the world then bring it to PyCon SK 2019 :-)

You can find some inspiration here www.ucimeshardverom.sk/projekty

Teaching with Hardware

The goal of the initiative Teaching with Hardware is to introduce such activities into computing lessons at primary and secondary schools, which will familiarise students with interesting applications of programming and information technologies, thus motivating them in developing their digital literacy and algorithmic thinking. We do this by introducing physical computing with a small programmable device called BBC micro:bit, and by supporting teachers with methodical materials and teacher training. In october 2018 we started a PILOT project on 14 schools across Slovakia, and the results will be announced at PyCon SK 2019.

More info can be found at www.ucimeshardverom.sk
